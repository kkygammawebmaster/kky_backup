Big Brother Experience

Published by admin on April 16, 2018

By Dylan Majewsky

Husky fans storm the court after Dominic Green’s game-winning three-point shot at the buzzer for a 78-75 upset against #9 Arizona, a team under fire this past season for its corruption scandal against Arizona star player DeAndre Ayton. Photo Credit: Dustine Bowker

This season’s UW basketball was a role reversal from the year before: the men are now shooting for the stars while the women‘s performance seems lackluster.

The last season was Women's basketball winningest of all time, with star senior Kelsey Plum leading the team to the Sweet Sixteen. But soon after it ended, coach Mike Neighbors left for Arkansas, leaving the role of women’s basketball coach open. Many key players left as well, leaving only five returning players, with a total roster of ten. Jody Wynn stepped up and began her first season as the women's basketball coach, having come from Long Beach State. With a tough schedule, the Huskies did not perform well, and Fapou Semebene’s early injury did not help. Despite having great players Jenna Moser and Amber Melgoza, the women’s team ended the season in a close loss to Cal in the first round of the PAC-12 tournament, 68-71. The final overall record was 7-23, 1-17 in conference.

Mike Hopkins began his first season as the Men's basketball new coach after previous coach Lorenzo Romar was fired after years of poor records. Hopkins made the choice to leave his 22 season role of assistant coach at Syracuse It also led to the decommitment of Michael Porter Jr., a five-star prospect. Hopkins hoped to add new energy and determination to the team. His work showed early in the season, as the Huskies unexpectedly beat #2 Kansas on the road 74-65, though they lost the next game to #12 Gonzaga at home 70-97. After making a good record of the season so far, we beat the controversial #9 Arizona at home with Romar present as Arizona’s associate head coach in a buzzer beater by Dominic Green 78-75. Later in the season, Isaiah Thomas's #2 jersey was retired. Unfortunately, the Huskies lost in the first round of the PAC-12 tournament vs. Oregon State after having beat them only a week before in conference play. It ruined the chance chance of entering the NCAA tournament, having not made it since 2011. Afterwards, the Huskies lost in the second game of the NIT vs Saint Mary’s (California) 81-85. They finished the season with an overall record 21-13, 10-8 in conference. Afterwards, Hopkins won PAC-12 Coach of the year.
