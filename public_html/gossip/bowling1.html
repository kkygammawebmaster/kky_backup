Bowling Tip Series #1 - Consistency

Published by Austin Quach on May 3, 2018

By Dustine Bowker

The individual strips of wood on the lane are the boards. Each dot is in line with the arrows, which are on every fifth board of the lane.

Bowling is a game of consistency; ideally, youâ€™ll want to find a specific target on the lane and stick with it. While the goal is to knock down as many pins as possible, know that there are other ways to help you find your target. You can use any one of the seven arrows on the lane, or even the individual boards, which are the individual lines of wood that you see if you look at the bowling lane up close. The boards can help you guide where you want to aim for.

Having a system for getting strikes and all sorts of spares is crucial. However, it is not possible to get strikes and spares consistently without having a relaxed arm swing. When rolling the ball, let gravity take care of the rest of the roll. Think of it this way: you can use gravity to create a pendulum-like motion with the bowling ball in your hand. This ensures that you are more likely to roll your bowling ball over the same target as consistently as possible. If you want to increase your bowling speed, begin with your bowling ball in a higher position before you take your approach to the foul line and bowl. Conversely, having your bowling ball in a lower position will enable a slower ball speed.

Consistency is ideal when bowling. Outside of consistency, there are other factors involved that make bowling a far more challenging sport than it may appear, including the oil pattern and surface of the lane and the surface of the bowling ball, among others. I will plan on discussing these aspects of bowling in my next article, including the oil patterns that you should expect on the lanes.

Dustine Bowker is a senior at UW who bowls in one of the two Gamma bowling teams this spring quarter as a part of the weekly HUB Bowling League. He holds a 217 average at the time of this article. He bowled competitively from the age of five through high school, and joined the HUB Bowling League during his junior year at UW.
