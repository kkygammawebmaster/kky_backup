A Blast from the (Not So Long Ago) Past

Published by Austin on May 16, 2018

Left to right: Nicholas Sianghio, Brian Schunke, Nicole Pasia, Austin Quach

A couple of weeks ago, I sat down with family founder Brian Schunke for a casual dinner at Cafe on the Ave. I wanted hear a first-hand account of Gamma as a recently reactivated chapter. Schunke was initiated with the Delta class as a sophomore in the spring of 1995. At that time, the chapter was so small, two classes were put through each year to accumulate a substantial number of active brothers. Even so, only two out of the 10 members of Schunke’s class returned for a second year, partly due to the financial strain of paying both membership and yearly dues in a short amount of time. Along with the degrees of the spring class were, of course, elections for the next year’s Executive Council. Due to a strange timing of events, Schunke was actually elected chapter President before formally going through 3rd degree.

Despite its initial struggles as a newly active chapter, Gamma’s active resilience has persevered for over two decades. Schunke went on to share some of his favorite anecdotes, spanning from various road trips to district and national events, to prominent fraternity members from his time as an active brother.

As our interview came to a close, I asked Schunke for his thoughts on Gamma’s future. With over 60 active brothers, Schunke felt confident in our chapter’s ability to maintain itself and continue to put on various projects throughout the year. He would also like to see new projects taken on by Gamma, such as increased involvement leading up to Band Day. I would like to thank Brian Schunke for helping me, and the rest of Gamma, get a better sense of our chapter’s past. The impacts that Gamma’s alumni have left us are an integral part of not only our chapter’s history, but our present and future as well. May our past continue to inspire us to always strive for the highest!
